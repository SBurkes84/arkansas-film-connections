Featured Films

Films with Arkansas Connections

These eight titles are examples of films and series that connect to Arkansas in different ways: they may have been filmed in Arkansas, feature Arkansas actors, or reference Arkansas history and culture.

Drama

Sling Blade (1996)

Written, directed, and headlined by Billy Bob Thornton, this film follows Karl Childers after his release from a psychiatric hospital as he tries to adjust to small-town life.

Arkansas connection: Thornton is from Arkansas, and the film was shot in the state. It also earned him an Academy Award.

Billy Bob Thornton Academy Award

Drama

Mud (2012)

Two boys growing up along the Mississippi River discover a fugitive named Mud hiding on a river island. As they help him, they are pulled into his complicated past.

Arkansas connection: Filmed in Arkansas and tied to actor Wes Bentley’s career, this movie brought more attention to Arkansas riverfront locations.

Wes Bentley Filmed in Arkansas

Romantic Comedy / Drama

Chasing Amy (1997)

A story about relationships, friendship, and identity in the comic book industry. Joey Lauren Adams plays a key lead role that helped shape her career.

Arkansas connection: Adams is an Arkansas native and was widely praised for her performance in this film.

Joey Lauren Adams Arkansas Native

Drama / Comedy

Melvin and Howard (1980)

Based loosely on a true story involving an unexpected connection to billionaire Howard Hughes, this film focuses on a working-class man whose life changes after a strange encounter.

Arkansas connection: Mary Steenburgen, who is from Arkansas, won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for her role in this movie.

Mary Steenburgen Academy Award

Science Fiction TV

Buck Rogers in the 25th Century (1979–1981)

A science fiction television series that followed the adventures of Buck Rogers after he wakes up 500 years in the future.

Arkansas connection: Gil Gerard, the star of the series, is from Little Rock, Arkansas, and became known for this role.

Gil Gerard Little Rock

Legal Thriller

The Firm (1993)

A legal thriller based on the John Grisham novel, following a young lawyer who discovers that his new law firm is hiding dangerous secrets.

Arkansas connection: Although much of the film is set in Memphis, several scenes were filmed on the Arkansas side of the river, showcasing regional locations.

John Grisham Story Regional Filming

Biographical Drama

Walk the Line (2005)

A biopic about the life of musician Johnny Cash, focusing on his early years, rise to fame, and relationship with June Carter.

Arkansas connection: Cash was born in Arkansas, and parts of the movie were filmed in Arkansas to reflect his roots.

Johnny Cash Arkansas Roots

Western

True Grit (2010)

A reimagining of the classic western about a young girl seeking justice for her father’s death with the help of a U.S. Marshal.

Arkansas connection: Portions of the film were shot in Arkansas and the story connects to regional frontier history and landscapes.

Western Regional Landscapes

Cast & Creators

Actor & Arkansas Connections

Here are some of the key performers and figures tied to these films and to Arkansas. Mapping these people helped me see how often Arkansas shows up on screen, even when the story is set somewhere else.

  • Billy Bob Thornton – Arkansas native; wrote, directed, and starred in Sling Blade.
  • Wes Bentley – Born in Arkansas; appears in Mud and other major films.
  • Joey Lauren Adams – Arkansas native; praised for her leading role in Chasing Amy.
  • Mary Steenburgen – From Arkansas; won an Academy Award for Melvin and Howard.
  • Gil Gerard – Sci-fi actor from Little Rock; starred in Buck Rogers in the 25th Century.
  • Johnny Cash – Born in Kingsland, Arkansas; his life story is central to Walk the Line.
  • Matthew McConaughey – Lead actor in Mud, one of the most recognized Arkansas-filmed projects.
  • Reese Witherspoon – Starred in Walk the Line, portraying June Carter alongside Johnny Cash’s Arkansas story.
  • Joaquin Phoenix – Played Johnny Cash in Walk the Line, bringing Arkansas music history to the screen.
  • Jeff Bridges – Starred in the 2010 version of True Grit, tied to frontier culture connected to Arkansas history.
  • Hailee Steinfeld – Had her breakout role in True Grit; the story connects to regional frontier themes.
  • Tom Cruise – Leading role in The Firm, which featured scenes shot on Arkansas riverfront locations.

Director’s note

I treated this section like a connection map. Instead of just listing names, I made sure each person is tied back to a specific project and place in Arkansas. It keeps the page readable but still shows how wide the network of Arkansas talent really is.

Behind the Scenes

About this project

This project was built over the course of a semester as part of my Capstone 2 class. I started with a simple idea—shine a light on Arkansas films and actors—then broke it down into pages, sections, and data I could actually manage.

From there, I focused on structure first and style second. Once the content was organized, I layered on design choices like gradients, motion, and highlight cards so the site feels like a small movie experience instead of just another list of links.

I prepared this page to be hosted on GitHub so I can point to it as proof that I can plan, design, and ship a complete front-end project on my own—from idea, to layout, to final polish.
